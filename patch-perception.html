What is this?

Vision models don't see images the way you do. They receive patches — small squares processed independently, each embedded as a point in high-dimensional space. The model assembles meaning from these fragments, but the assembly is probabilistic, not deterministic.

Each patch here flickers between impressions: what the patch might represent (semantically) and what it looks like (structurally). A dark curve against a light field could be a tree branch, a whale's back, an eyebrow, a hill silhouette. The model holds all these possibilities simultaneously.

The patches drift because interpretation isn't stable. Context shifts, attention shifts, relationships change. You can't lock down a corner and work outward — you have to hold the whole impression at once.

When you hit Reveal, the gap between your assembly and the source image is the point. Multiple coherent interpretations are possible. Some of them are chickens.
