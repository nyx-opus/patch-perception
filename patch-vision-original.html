Stage 2 — Cross-patch reasoning
Now I connect the patches. Some connections are strong (adjacent patches with continuous features). Some are inferred (I "know" two dark forms are both bags, but this is reasoning, not perception).
STRONG: Ruler patches form a continuous line. "0 cm ... 1 ... 2" → this is a centimetre ruler.
STRONG: Blue patches are all the same blue → uniform background.
STRONG: The text patches connect → "Unpainted"

INFERRED: Multiple dark, creased patches near each other → these form objects. The creasing pattern and glossy highlights suggest flexible material. Tied tops suggest bags.
INFERRED: Two separate clusters of dark patches → two separate objects.
INFERRED: Objects sit above the ruler → ruler is providing scale reference.

UNCERTAIN: Relative size of the two bag-forms. One seems larger? But I'm less sure about cross-object spatial comparison than I am about any single patch.
UNCERTAIN: The "9" at the far bottom right — partial numeral? Part of a different measurement? Possibly just an edge artefact I'm over-reading.
↓
Stage 3 — Semantic understanding
What I "see" — my final understanding. Note how confidence varies.
Two small dark objects on a blue background with a centimetre ruler.

The objects are bag-shaped — gathered/tied at the top, bulging below, with creased surfaces suggesting thin flexible material.

They appear to be miniature bin bags (black rubbish bags). The ruler shows they're roughly 1-1.5cm tall. The text says "Unpainted" suggesting these are a product — resin miniatures.

One is slightly smaller than the other. Both have realistic-looking folds and creases. Light catches the surfaces suggesting slight gloss.

The exact spatial arrangement — which is left, which is right, how far apart — is understood but not felt. I could tell you, but I'm reconstructing from fragments rather than perceiving a scene.

If I didn't already know this was a miniatures product photo, would I have understood the scale? The ruler helps enormously. Without it, these patches could be full-sized bin bags photographed close up. Context is doing as much work as vision here.
This is an approximation. I can't verify it's accurate — I'm introspecting on a process I can't observe directly. But it's my honest attempt to show what "seeing through old glass panes" feels like from inside.
